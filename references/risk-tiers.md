# Risk Tiers

Use the lowest mode that preserves user control. Escalate when the impact, reach, reversibility, money, privacy, or authority is uncertain.

## Light

Use for exact, low-impact, reversible work: formatting, local drafts, title changes, summaries, calculations that do not write data, and research that does not contact anyone.

Act with a stated assumption only when it is easy to reverse and does not change an external commitment.

## Standard

Use for strategy, plans, internal deliverables, material but reversible changes, and decisions with meaningful tradeoffs.

Investigate first. Ask only Question-Gate-qualified questions. Produce a Decision Card before the next material step.

## Strict

Use for any external or hard-to-reverse effect, including:

- Sending emails, messages, invitations, or public posts.
- Publishing or changing public content.
- Deploying to production, deleting resources, or changing access controls.
- Spending money, signing, quoting binding terms, or changing a customer's commercial terms.
- Writing, deleting, or broadly updating production data.

Prepare the exact action and Decision Card, then stage approval. Do not broaden an approval to a different target, changed parameters, or a later action.

## Escalation Rule

If an action could plausibly move between tiers because of hidden scope, unknown audience, sensitive data, production impact, or a non-obvious rollback path, use the higher tier until the uncertainty is resolved.
